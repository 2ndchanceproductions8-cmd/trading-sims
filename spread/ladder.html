Why this bot needs a ladder at all

The margin model reads season-average scoring rates. A trade doesn't move a season average by a single point — so the day after a team ships its leading scorer, the model's expected margin is identical to the day before, while the market reprices the spread in minutes. A spread is a bet on the difference between two rosters, so a stale rate on either side lands straight on the number.
  • Ceiling — reject any edge above +10%. A season scoring average does not find a 15% edge against a priced spread; that is the model being wrong, not the market.
  • Form — recency-weighted scoring rates blended 50/50 with the season rate. Adapts to a changed roster in weeks instead of never. Shrink is identical to the control's, so only the rate moves.
  • Clean — no bet when either side traded/released inside 3 days or has 3+ fresh significant injuries.
  • Sharp — quarter-Kelly staking instead of flat 2%.
Honest note: sigma, home-field edge, the form blend and the lookback windows are standard public estimates, not fitted. This ladder is what judges them. Don't cite them as tuned.
