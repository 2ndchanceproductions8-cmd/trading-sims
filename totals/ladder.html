Why this bot needs a ladder at all

The model reads season-average scoring rates. A trade doesn't move a season average by a single point — so the day after a team ships its leading scorer (or its ace), the model's expected total is identical to the day before, while the market reprices in minutes. Totals are more personnel-driven than moneylines: losing one starting pitcher moves a run line further than it moves who wins. A stale rate is a bigger error here, not a smaller one.
  • Ceiling — reject any edge above +10%. A season scoring average does not find a 15% edge; that is the model being wrong.
  • Form — recency-weighted scoring rates blended 50/50 with the season rate. Adapts to a changed roster in weeks instead of never. Shrink is identical to the control's, so only the rate moves.
  • Clean — no bet when either side traded/released inside 3 days or has 3+ fresh significant injuries. A total is a matchup of two scoring rates, so either roster poisons it.
  • Sharp — quarter-Kelly staking instead of flat 2%.
Honest note: the form blend and lookback windows are not fit — there was no way to backtest them at build time. They're reasonable defaults, and this ladder is what will judge them. Don't cite them as tuned.
