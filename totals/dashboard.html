How the model reads a total

Each team's scoring rate and rate-allowed come from ESPN's standings, then get regressed toward the league average (a hot week isn't a rate). Offense then meets defense: exp_home = home_off × away_def / lg_avg, same for the away side, added together. A normal curve around that expected total — with a per-sport spread — gives P(over), which is compared against the over/under price with the vig stripped out.
The known blind spot: season averages don't know who is pitching tonight. In MLB the starting pitcher moves a run total more than either team's average does, and the market prices him — this model can't see him. Expect MLB to be the weakest league on this board. That's the experiment, not a bug.
