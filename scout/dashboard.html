🧒 The whole game in 5 rules

1
🏪 Only shop from the big famous shelf
The bot only ever looks at 30 huge, well-known companies (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia…). Big companies are easy to sell out of at any moment — tiny mystery stocks can trap your money.
2
📈 Only buy a line that is already climbing
Look at the chart for the last 5 days (the shaded part on every chart below). Higher than a week ago → it may keep going. Lower → walk away. Never try to catch a falling toy — it usually keeps falling.
3
📰 Only buy when the stories are happy
Read the newest headlines about the company and count happy words (beats, record, upgrade) against sad words (miss, lawsuit, layoffs). The happy words must clearly win — a little happy is not enough.
4
✋ Never bet big, even when you feel sure
At most a quarter of the piggy bank ($2500 of $10000) on any one stock. At most 2 buys a day and 4 stocks owned at once. Feeling sure is a feeling, not a fact.
5
📏 Draw both lines BEFORE you buy — then obey them
The moment you buy, draw the 😣 ouch line -5% below your price and the 🎉 win line +10% above it. Price touches either one → sell. No feelings, no "maybe it comes back". Nothing happened for 14 days → sell too. And if the whole piggy bank ever drops to $9000, stop playing completely — the game just told you it doesn't work.

✍️ Do it yourself one day — the exact checklist

This is everything the bot does, written as a ~15-minute morning routine. Practice for free: predict what the bot will do, then check this page the next day and see if you matched it.

  1. Open a 1-month chart of one big famous company (use the bot's table above for the list). Is today's price higher than 5 trading days ago? No → walk away. Yes → step 2.
  2. Read the newest headlines about it — the bot reads about 8. Clearly more happy news than sad? No → walk away. Yes → step 3.
  3. Decide your ticket BEFORE buying: never more than 25% of your money. With $10,000 that means $2,500, maximum. Write the number down.
  4. Write down two prices BEFORE buying: ouch = price × 0.95 and win = price × 1.10.
  5. Buy, then IMMEDIATELY set your protection: place a stop-loss sell order at the ouch price (that's an order that sells automatically for you if the price ever falls there — the app does the discipline). Robinhood won't let you ALSO place the win-price sell order on the same shares, so set a price alert at the win number instead — when it rings, sell that day. Then close the app.
  6. Hard limits, no exceptions, ever: max 2 buys a day, max 4 stocks at once, sell anything you've held 14 days, and if the news about something you own turns clearly bad, sell it that day. If your account ever drops 10% total, STOP completely — like the bot does — and don't restart without a written plan for what you'll do differently.

The secret nobody likes: the steps are easy — obeying them when your gut screams "but it will come back!" is the entire skill. That is why we watch the bot do it without feelings first, and why the orders in step 5 get placed before emotions show up.
