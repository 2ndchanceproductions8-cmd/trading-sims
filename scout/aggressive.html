🔥 How the twin plays — in 5 simple rules

1
🎯 One bet — the very best one
It puts ALL its money on ONE stock, and only a super-strong one: a top score (≥ 5), happy news, AND the price going up today and over the week. If nothing is that strong, it buys nothing at all.
2
✂️ Snip a loser FAST
If the price drops 4% below what it paid, it sells right away — even faster than the careful bot's 5%. Tiny cuts never grow into big holes.
3
🚀 Let a winner FLY
It has NO win line. While a stock keeps climbing, it keeps holding. That's the whole point: it's hunting the one rare BIG winner, not lots of little ones.
4
🪂 A safety net that climbs with the kite
Instead of a win line, it keeps a moving net 6% under the highest price so far. Every time the price makes a new high, the net lifts up too — so it keeps most of a big win but can never give the whole thing back.
5
🔄 Always ride the hottest
If its stock stops climbing, or a clearly hotter super-strong one appears (+3 more points), it switches. It never sits on a cold stock — and it has no "stop playing" floor.
⚠️ The catch — say it out loud: one big bet means every win AND every mistake is about 4× bigger than the careful bot. One bad morning can wipe out weeks of gains. That's exactly why the careful bot exists, and why this twin is a race to watch and learn from — not a plan to copy with money you can't lose.

✍️ Do the twin's way yourself — the checklist

Five steps, same as the bot. The hard part isn't the steps — it's holding a winner without getting scared, and snipping a loser without hoping. Watch the bot do it without feelings first.

  1. Find the ONE strongest stock today: top score, happy news, and going up both today AND over the week. If nothing is clearly that strong, do nothing — that's allowed, and it's smart.
  2. Buy it with your set-aside play money. (The twin bets all of its pretend cash; in real life only ever use money you can fully afford to lose.)
  3. Right away, set a stop-loss sell at price × 0.96 (-4%). That's your 'snip a loser fast' net.
  4. Do NOT set a win price. Instead, each morning move your stop UP to (highest price so far) × 0.94. The net follows the kite up and never moves down.
  5. Sell when price hits that climbing net — OR when the stock stops being strong — OR when a clearly hotter one shows up. Then repeat with the new best stock.

Reminder: this style swings big both ways. Only the careful bot's way is safe for money you actually need. This page exists to teach the idea, not to talk you into it.
