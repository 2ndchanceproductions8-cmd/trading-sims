🧒 The whole game in 6 rules

1
🏪 Only shop from the big famous shelf
The bot only ever looks at 30 huge, well-known companies (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia…). Big companies are easy to get in and out of at any moment — long or short.
2
🌊 Swim WITH the tide, never against it
Every morning it first checks the whole market (SPY vs its 50-day average). Market clearly rising → no new shorts. Market clearly falling → no new longs. Most losing trades come from fighting the tape. Today's tide: MIXED.
3
↕️ Two ways to win — but each has its own rules
Strong score + happy news + climbing price → buy it (long). Strongly negative score + sad news + falling price that's already below its own 20-day average → short it (borrow-and-sell, profit if it keeps falling). Never short a stock that's still rising — that's how squeezes eat accounts.
4
✋ Never bet big, even when you feel sure
At most 20% of the piggy bank on any one idea, at most 2 new positions a day, 5 open at once. Shorts reserve their full value in cash as collateral — no leverage, ever.
5
📏 Draw both lines BEFORE you enter — then obey them
The moment it enters, the 😣 ouch line sits 5% against the position and the 🎉 win line +10% in its favor — for a short those lines are simply flipped (ouch is ABOVE, win is BELOW). Price touches either → exit. Nothing happens for 14 days → exit. Shorts also cover the day the news turns clearly happy — never sit in a squeeze.
6
⛔ Know when the game is over
If the whole piggy bank ever drops to $9,000 (-10%), stop playing completely — the experiment has answered. A strategy that can't protect 90% of its bankroll doesn't deserve the other 90%.

✍️ Do it yourself one day — the exact checklist

This is everything the bot does, written as a ~15-minute morning routine. Practice for free: predict what the bot will do, then check this page the next day and see if you matched it.

  1. Check the tide first: is SPY above or below its 50-day average, and which way has it drifted this week? Rising tide → only look for longs. Falling tide → only look for shorts (or sit out). Never fight it.
  2. Open a 1-month chart of one big famous company. LONG idea: is it higher than 5 trading days ago? SHORT idea: lower than 5 days ago AND below its 20-day average? No → walk away.
  3. Read the newest headlines — the bot reads about 8. For a long the happy words must clearly win; for a short the sad words must clearly win. Mixed news → walk away.
  4. Decide your ticket BEFORE entering: never more than 20% of your money. With $10,000 that's $2,000, maximum. Write it down.
  5. Write down two prices BEFORE entering. Long: ouch = price × 0.95, win = price × 1.10. Short: ouch = price × 1.05 (above!), win = price × 0.90 (below!). Enter, then immediately place the stop order at the ouch price and an alert at the win price. Then close the app.
  6. Hard limits, no exceptions: max 2 entries a day, max 5 positions at once, exit anything held 14 days, cover any short the day its news turns happy, and if your account ever drops 10% total, STOP completely.
  7. One honest warning about real shorting: a long can only lose what you put in; a short's loss is unlimited if the price keeps rising, and brokers charge borrow fees. That's why the bot proves the idea with pretend money and cash-secured rules first.

The secret nobody likes: the steps are easy — obeying them when your gut screams "but it will come back!" is the entire skill. Shorts test that discipline twice as hard, because a rising loser FEELS like it must fall any minute. It doesn't have to. The ouch line is the only opinion that counts.
