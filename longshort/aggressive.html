🔥 How the twin plays — in 5 simple rules

1
🎯 One bet — the very best one, EITHER direction
It puts ALL its money on ONE stock: the strongest setup on the board, long or short (|score| ≥ 5). A rocketing stock with happy news → all-in long. A collapsing stock with sad news, already below its 20-day average → all-in short. Nothing that strong → it buys nothing at all.
2
✂️ Snip a loser FAST
If the price moves 4% AGAINST it (down for a long, UP for a short), it exits right away — even faster than the careful bot's 5%. Tiny cuts never grow into big holes — and for a short, a fast cut is also squeeze insurance.
3
🚀 Let a winner FLY
It has NO win line. While a long keeps climbing — or a short keeps sinking — it keeps holding. It's hunting the one rare BIG move, not lots of little ones.
4
🪂 A safety net that moves with the trade
Instead of a win line, it keeps a moving net 6% off the BEST price seen so far — below the peak for a long, ABOVE the lowest low for a short. The net only ever moves in the trade's favor, so it keeps most of a big win but can never give the whole thing back.
5
🔄 Always ride the strongest
If its trade stops working, or a clearly stronger A+ setup appears (+3 more |score| points, either direction), it switches. It never sits on a dead trade — and it has no "stop playing" floor.
⚠️ The catch — say it out loud: one big bet means every win AND every mistake is about 5× bigger than the careful bot's. And an all-in SHORT is the single most dangerous trade in markets — in real life a short squeeze can lose MORE than everything, which is why this is a simulation to watch and learn from, never a plan to copy with money you can't lose.

✍️ Do the twin's way yourself — the checklist

Five steps, same as the bot. The hard part isn't the steps — it's holding a winner without getting scared, and snipping a loser without hoping. Shorts double the difficulty: a short that rises FEELS like it must fall soon. It doesn't have to.

  1. Find the ONE strongest setup today, either direction: for a long — top score, happy news, up today AND on the week. For a short — deeply negative score, sad news, down today AND on the week, already below its 20-day average. Nothing that strong → do nothing. That's allowed.
  2. Enter it with your set-aside play money. (The twin bets all of its pretend cash; in real life only ever use money you can fully afford to lose — and remember a real short can lose MORE than you put in.)
  3. Right away, set the exit stop 4% AGAINST the trade: long → sell-stop at price × 0.96; short → buy-stop at price × 1.04. That's your 'snip a loser fast' net.
  4. Do NOT set a win price. Each morning move the stop to 6% off the BEST price so far — below the peak for a long, above the lowest low for a short. The net only moves in your favor, never back.
  5. Exit when price hits the moving net — OR when the move stops being strong — OR when a clearly stronger setup shows up. Then repeat.

Reminder: this style swings big both ways, and all-in shorting is the most dangerous version of it. Only the careful bot's way is remotely sane for money you actually need. This page exists to teach the idea, not to talk you into it.
